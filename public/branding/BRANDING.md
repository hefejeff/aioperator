# West Monroe Brand Guidelines

This document provides a comprehensive overview of the West Monroe brand identity, including our philosophy, visual elements, and voice. Adhering to these guidelines ensures a consistent and professional brand experience across all communications.

---

## 1. Brand Overview

### Core Philosophy
Our brand is built on being **clear and human**. We aim to communicate complex ideas in a way that is direct, accessible, and impactful. Our visual and written identity should always be grounded, professional, and approachable.

### Central Resource
The official **Brand Compass (brand.westmonroe.com)** and **Templafy** are the primary sources for all brand assets, including templates, logos, photos, and design elements.

---

## 2. Color Palette

Our color palette is foundational to our brand identity. It consists of primary, highlight, accent, and neutral colors used to create structure, emphasis, and visual interest.

### Primary Palette
These colors form the backbone of our templates and primary communications.

| Color | Hex Code | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Grounded Blue | `#000033` | Primary text, logos, key graphic elements. |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Backgrounds, negative space, reverse text. |

### Highlight Color
This color is used exclusively for a specific graphic element to draw attention to key words.

| Color | Hex Code | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Highlight Yellow | `#F2E800` | **Strictly for the "highlight graphic"** to spotlight impactful words. Do not use for text, backgrounds, or other elements. |

### Accent & Neutral Palette
These colors are used to differentiate content, emphasize key information, and organize layouts.

| Color | Hex Code | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Accent Blue | `#0045FF` | Emphasize primary subheads, key phrases, text, and illustrations. |
| Accent Pink | `#F500A0` | Emphasize select primary subheads, stats, illustrations, and numbered lists. |
| Support Neutral | `#CBD2DA` | Organizing content and graphics, such as backgrounds for charts or call-outs. |

---

## 3. Typography

Our typography is clean, modern, and highly legible, reflecting our "clear and human" voice.

### Font Family
- **Primary Font**: Arial

### Hierarchy & Usage
- **Headlines**: Use **Arial Bold**. Keep headlines concise, ideally sticking to a maximum of two lines. Use a large font size and a strong color like Grounded Blue.
- **Subheads**: Use Arial Bold or a standard weight. Accent colors can be used for emphasis on primary subheads.
- **Body Copy**: Use Arial Regular. Ensure ample line spacing for readability. Use for descriptions, bullet points, and longer text passages.
- **Emphasis**: Use **bold** weight or accent colors to highlight key phrases or stats within the body copy.

---

## 4. Logo Usage

The West Monroe logo is our primary brand identifier and must be used consistently.

### Primary Logo
The logo consists of the symbol mark and the lowercase "west monroe" wordmark.

### Rules
- **Clear Space**: Always maintain a generous amount of clear space around the logo. This space should be free of any other text, graphics, or distracting elements.
- **Sizing**: Ensure the logo is always reproduced at a size where the "west monroe" wordmark is clearly legible. Avoid scaling the logo down to a point where it becomes unreadable.
- **Placement**: The logo is typically placed in the top-left corner of a composition, as seen in our standard templates.

### Do's & Don'ts
- **DO**: Use the primary Grounded Blue logo on a white or light-colored background.
- **DO**: Ensure proper clear space is maintained.
- **DON'T**: Stretch, distort, rotate, or alter the logo in any way.
- **DON'T**: Recolor the logo outside of approved primary brand colors (e.g., a white reverse version for dark backgrounds).
- **DON'T**: Place the logo on a cluttered or visually complex background.

---

## 5. Imagery

Our imagery helps to tell our story, visualize data, and add a personal touch to our communications.

### Photography
- **Style**: Use natural and abstract photos that show our work in action. Photography should feel authentic, professional, and human-centric.

### Illustrations
- **Purpose**: Use illustrations to break down and visualize complex ideas, making them easier to understand.
- **Color**: Accent colors can be used within illustrations to emphasize key components.

### Icons
- **Purpose**: Use icons to complement your message, especially in infographics or to symbolize a concept. They should be clean, simple, and stylistically consistent.

---

## 6. Layout & Composition

Our layouts are structured, clean, and designed to guide the audience through the content logically.

### Templates
- Always start with an official **Brand Compass** or **Templafy** template. The backbone of our templates is the primary color palette of Grounded Blue and White.

### Grid & Spacing
- Use a clean, organized grid system. Align elements logically (e.g., strong left-alignment for text).
- Be generous with white space to avoid clutter and improve readability.

### Common Layout Elements
- **Headers**: Every slide or document should have a clear, powerful header that states the big idea.
- **Highlight Graphic**: Use the built-in highlight graphic layout to spotlight impactful words in key messages.
- **Content Blocks**: Use subheads and bullet points to quickly convey key concepts.
- **Call-outs**: Use square call-out boxes to pull out and emphasize big ideas or important quotes.
- **Data Visualization**: Use tables and graphs to present hard numbers and complex data. Leverage the accent and neutral color palette for clarity in data visualization.

---

## 7. Voice & Tone

Our brand voice is a direct reflection of our core philosophy.

### Core Attributes
- **Clear**: We use simple, direct language. We avoid jargon and ambiguity.
- **Human**: We are approachable and relatable. Our writing should sound like a conversation with a trusted, knowledgeable expert.

### Application
- **Be Direct**: Start with what you want to say. Get straight to the point.
- **Be Helpful**: Guide the reader through the information.
- **Be Confident**: Write with authority, leveraging data and facts to support our message.
